A Homeowner's Guide to Understanding and Using Home Equity

What is Home Equity?

In simple terms, home equity is the portion of your home that you own outright. It's the difference between your home's current market value and the amount you still owe on your mortgage. Think of it as your financial stake in your property.

  • Building Equity Through Payments: Every time you make a mortgage payment, a portion of it goes toward reducing your principal balance, which directly increases your equity.
  • Building Equity Through Appreciation: As the real estate market grows, your home's value may increase. This appreciation also adds to your total equity without you having to do anything.

How to Access Your Home Equity

Once you've built up a significant amount of equity, you can borrow against it. This is typically done through two main products:

  • Home Equity Loan: A lump-sum loan with a fixed interest rate and a set repayment period. It's great for large, one-time expenses.
  • Home Equity Line of Credit (HELOC): A flexible line of credit with a variable interest rate. You can draw and repay funds as needed during a "draw period," making it ideal for ongoing projects or as an emergency fund. Our calculator helps you estimate your potential HELOC limit.

Smart Ways to Use Your Home Equity

Leveraging your home equity can be a powerful financial move, but it's important to be strategic. Since you are securing the loan with your home, it's best to use the funds for things that provide a good return on investment.

  • Home Renovations: Using a HELOC to remodel your kitchen or add a bathroom can increase your home's value, effectively paying back some of the investment.
  • Debt Consolidation: Pay off high-interest credit cards or personal loans. The interest rate on a HELOC is typically much lower, which can save you a significant amount of money and simplify your monthly bills.
  • Education Expenses: Funding a college degree or certification can be a smart investment in your or your family's future earning potential.
