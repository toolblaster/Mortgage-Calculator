Why Paying Off Your Mortgage Early Makes Sense

When you take out a mortgage, you're not just paying back the loan; you're paying interest on that loan for up to 30 years. The longer the term, the more interest you pay. For example, a $400,000 loan at 6.5% can cost over $500,000 in interest if paid over 30 years—that's more than the original loan amount!

  • Massive Interest Savings: Every extra dollar you pay toward the principal is a dollar you won't pay interest on for the remaining life of the loan.
  • Financial Freedom: Eliminating your largest monthly expense frees up hundreds or thousands of dollars for investing, travel, or early retirement.
  • Peace of Mind: Owning your home outright provides an incredible sense of security and reduces financial stress.

5 Proven Strategies to Pay Off Your Mortgage Faster

1. Make Extra Monthly Payments

This is the simplest and most common strategy. By adding even a small, consistent amount to your regular payment, you can make a huge impact. To find out how extra payments shorten your payoff time, use our Extra Payment Calculator.

2. Switch to Biweekly Payments

With a biweekly plan, you pay half of your monthly mortgage payment every two weeks. Because there are 26 two-week periods in a year, you end up making 13 full monthly payments instead of 12. That one extra payment goes directly to your principal and can shorten your loan term by several years.

3. Refinance to a Shorter-Term Loan

If your financial situation has improved, consider refinancing from a 30-year to a 15-year mortgage. Your monthly payments will be higher, but you'll get a lower interest rate and pay off the loan in half the time. Compare how refinancing could lower your term with our dedicated calculator.

4. Apply Windfalls or Bonuses Toward the Principal

Whenever you receive a financial windfall—like a tax refund, a work bonus, or an inheritance—resist the urge to spend it all. Applying a lump sum directly to your principal can instantly shave months or even years off your loan. Be sure to instruct your lender to apply it to "principal only."

5. Cut Expenses and Reinvest the Savings

Take a close look at your budget. Can you cut back on subscriptions, dining out, or other non-essential expenses? Redirecting even $50-$100 per month from your budget to your mortgage principal adds up significantly over time.

Should You Pay Off Your Mortgage Early or Invest?

This is a common financial debate. Paying off your mortgage provides a guaranteed, risk-free return equal to your interest rate. Investing, on the other hand, offers the potential for higher returns but comes with risk. To make the best decision, check your overall financial health with our Affordability Calculator or estimate your monthly payments here to see how much room you have in your budget.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not Checking for Prepayment Penalties: Some loans charge a fee if you pay off too much too soon. Always check your loan documents first.
  • Ignoring Your Emergency Fund: Before paying extra on your mortgage, make sure you have 3-6 months of living expenses saved in an easily accessible account.
  • Forgetting About Liquidity: Money paid into your mortgage is not easy to get back. Make sure you have enough cash on hand for other life goals before tying it all up in your home.

FAQs About Paying Off Your Mortgage Early

How much faster can I pay off my mortgage with extra payments?

The time saved depends on your loan amount, interest rate, and how much extra you pay. For example, adding just $200 extra per month to a $300,000, 30-year loan could help you pay it off about 7 years sooner. Our Extra Payment Calculator can give you a precise answer for your specific loan.

Is it smart to pay off your mortgage early?

In many cases, yes. It provides a guaranteed 'return' on your money equal to your mortgage interest rate and offers significant peace of mind. However, if you have higher-interest debt (like credit cards) or believe you can earn a much higher return by investing, it might be better to put your extra cash there.

Does paying biweekly really save money?

Yes, an 'accelerated' biweekly plan saves a significant amount of money. By paying half your monthly payment every two weeks, you end up making 13 full payments a year instead of 12. That extra payment goes directly to the principal, which can shave years off your loan and reduce total interest.

Can I make one-time lump-sum payments?

Absolutely. Applying a lump sum from a bonus, tax refund, or inheritance directly to your principal is one of the most effective ways to reduce your loan balance and future interest payments. Just be sure to specify that the payment is for 'principal only'.
